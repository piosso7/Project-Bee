Feed the bees.

To increase bees chances for survive we can plant more flowers in our gardens, or even on windowsills. These actions will help a lot for sure!

Don't use pesticides.

You should use organic and ecological garden care products to keep bees safe and sound. Chemicals could cause harm and could be poisonous.

Buy from local bee breeders.

Shopping can be nice and also helpful. You can support local sellers by buying from them honey and other products. They are making everything in harmony with nature (not industrial).

The situation of bees.

Bee populations are rapidly declining around the world due to habitat loss, pollution and the use of pesticides, among other factors. “These creatures are vital to what we eat and what our countryside looks like,” says Gill Perkins, chief executive of the Bumblebee Conservation Trust.

How to protect the bees?

You can protect bees in other ways, for example: plant a bee garden; go chemical-free for bees; provide trees for bees; build homes for native bees; host a fundraiser; support local beekeepers and organizations.

Frequent Asked Questions

Bees are vital for the preservation of ecological balance and biodiversity in nature. They provide one of the most recognisable ecosystem services, i.e. pollination, which is what makes food production possible

Bees can be kept anywhere from country orchards to urban gardens to small city balconies. It is a common misconception that you need a large garden or countryside nearby. Although this can make siting your bees easier, urban gardens are arguably better.

Despite being what they are most associated with, did you know that not all bees actually make honey? Only honeybees specifically produce honey, as honey is their primary source of energy; it's their main food. There are only seven species of 'true' honey bees, which produce large amounts of honey.
